# Retro Command

## Purpose
Conduct comprehensive retrospectives to learn from development experiences, identify improvements, and continuously enhance development processes using systematic reflection and improvement methodologies.

## When to Use
- **Sprint Retrospectives**: When concluding development sprints or iterations
- **Project Retrospectives**: When completing projects or major milestones
- **Feature Retrospectives**: When completing features or major functionality
- **Incident Retrospectives**: When resolving critical issues or incidents
- **Process Retrospectives**: When evaluating and improving development processes
- **Team Retrospectives**: When assessing team performance and collaboration

## Command Structure

### Phase 1: Retrospective Planning & Preparation
**AUTONOMOUS PRINCIPAL ENGINEER DOCTRINE**: Begin with comprehensive planning and preparation for the retrospective.

#### Retrospective Planning
- **Scope Definition**: Define the scope and boundaries of the retrospective
- **Participant Identification**: Identify who should participate in the retrospective
- **Timeline Planning**: Plan the timeline and duration of the retrospective
- **Format Selection**: Choose appropriate retrospective format and methodology
- **Preparation Requirements**: Identify preparation requirements and materials

#### Data Collection
- **Performance Metrics**: Collect relevant performance metrics and data
- **Project Artifacts**: Gather project artifacts, documentation, and deliverables
- **Feedback Collection**: Collect feedback from stakeholders and team members
- **Issue Tracking**: Review issue tracking and resolution data
- **Process Documentation**: Review process documentation and procedures

### Phase 2: What Went Well Analysis
**Positive Reflection**: Identify and analyze what went well during the period.

#### Success Identification
- **Achievement Recognition**: Identify key achievements and successes
- **Process Successes**: Identify successful processes and methodologies
- **Team Successes**: Identify successful team collaboration and communication
- **Technical Successes**: Identify successful technical implementations
- **Business Successes**: Identify successful business outcomes and value delivery

#### Success Analysis
- **Success Factors**: Analyze factors that contributed to success
- **Best Practices**: Identify best practices that led to success
- **Replicable Patterns**: Identify patterns that can be replicated
- **Resource Utilization**: Analyze how resources were effectively utilized
- **Timeline Analysis**: Analyze how timelines were met or exceeded

### Phase 3: What Could Be Improved Analysis
**Improvement Identification**: Identify areas for improvement and learning opportunities.

#### Challenge Identification
- **Obstacle Recognition**: Identify obstacles and challenges encountered
- **Process Gaps**: Identify gaps in processes and procedures
- **Communication Issues**: Identify communication and collaboration issues
- **Technical Challenges**: Identify technical challenges and limitations
- **Resource Constraints**: Identify resource constraints and limitations

#### Improvement Opportunities
- **Process Improvements**: Identify opportunities for process improvement
- **Tool Improvements**: Identify opportunities for tool and technology improvement
- **Skill Development**: Identify opportunities for skill development and training
- **Communication Improvements**: Identify opportunities for better communication
- **Efficiency Improvements**: Identify opportunities for efficiency improvement

### Phase 4: Root Cause Analysis
**Systematic Investigation**: Analyze root causes of issues and challenges.

#### Issue Analysis
- **Problem Identification**: Identify specific problems and issues
- **Impact Assessment**: Assess the impact of identified problems
- **Timeline Analysis**: Analyze when and how problems occurred
- **Dependency Analysis**: Analyze dependencies and relationships
- **Pattern Recognition**: Identify patterns in problems and issues

#### Root Cause Investigation
- **Why Analysis**: Use "why" analysis to identify root causes
- **Fishbone Analysis**: Use fishbone diagrams to analyze causes
- **5 Whys Technique**: Use the 5 whys technique for root cause analysis
- **Systems Thinking**: Apply systems thinking to understand complex issues
- **Evidence-Based Analysis**: Base analysis on evidence and data

### Phase 5: Action Planning & Implementation
**Systematic Improvement**: Plan and implement improvements based on retrospective findings.

#### Action Planning
- **Improvement Prioritization**: Prioritize improvements based on impact and effort
- **Action Item Definition**: Define specific, actionable improvement items
- **Responsibility Assignment**: Assign responsibility for improvement actions
- **Timeline Planning**: Plan timelines for implementing improvements
- **Success Metrics**: Define metrics for measuring improvement success

#### Implementation Strategy
- **Quick Wins**: Identify and implement quick wins and low-hanging fruit
- **Long-term Improvements**: Plan for long-term, strategic improvements
- **Process Changes**: Implement process changes and improvements
- **Tool Upgrades**: Implement tool and technology upgrades
- **Training Programs**: Implement training and development programs

### Phase 6: Knowledge Capture & Sharing
**Knowledge Management**: Capture and share learnings from the retrospective.

#### Knowledge Capture
- **Lessons Learned**: Document key lessons learned and insights
- **Best Practices**: Document best practices and successful patterns
- **Process Improvements**: Document process improvements and changes
- **Tool Recommendations**: Document tool and technology recommendations
- **Training Needs**: Document training needs and development opportunities

#### Knowledge Sharing
- **Team Sharing**: Share learnings with team members
- **Stakeholder Communication**: Communicate learnings to stakeholders
- **Documentation Updates**: Update documentation with new learnings
- **Process Documentation**: Update process documentation with improvements
- **Training Materials**: Create training materials based on learnings

## Retrospective Methodologies

### Agile Retrospectives
- **Sprint-Based**: Conduct retrospectives at the end of each sprint
- **Team-Focused**: Focus on team collaboration and communication
- **Action-Oriented**: Emphasize actionable improvements and changes
- **Continuous Improvement**: Focus on continuous process improvement
- **Collaborative**: Encourage participation from all team members

### Project Retrospectives
- **Milestone-Based**: Conduct retrospectives at major project milestones
- **Comprehensive**: Cover all aspects of the project
- **Stakeholder-Inclusive**: Include all relevant stakeholders
- **Documentation-Heavy**: Emphasize comprehensive documentation
- **Strategic Focus**: Focus on strategic improvements and changes

### Incident Retrospectives
- **Incident-Focused**: Focus on specific incidents or critical issues
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Emphasize root cause analysis and prevention
- **Process Improvement**: Focus on process improvements to prevent recurrence
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Emphasize knowledge transfer and learning
- **Prevention Strategies**: Develop strategies to prevent similar incidents

## Retrospective Formats

### Traditional Retrospectives
- **What Went Well**: Identify successes and achievements
- **What Could Be Improved**: Identify areas for improvement
- **Action Items**: Define specific actions for improvement
- **Timeline**: Plan timeline for implementing improvements
- **Follow-up**: Plan follow-up and monitoring

### Modern Retrospectives
- **Start-Stop-Continue**: Identify what to start, stop, and continue
- **Mad-Sad-Glad**: Categorize experiences as mad, sad, or glad
- **4Ls**: Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for
- **Sailboat**: Identify anchors (what's slowing us down) and wind (what's helping us)
- **Temperature Reading**: Gauge team temperature and satisfaction

### Technology-Adaptive Retrospectives
- **Framework-Specific**: Adapt to specific technology frameworks
- **Tool-Focused**: Focus on tool and technology improvements
- **Process-Optimized**: Optimize for specific development processes
- **Team-Specific**: Adapt to team size and composition
- **Context-Sensitive**: Adapt to project context and requirements

## Integration with Other Commands

### Pre-Retrospective
- Use with `/analyze-project-deep` to understand project performance
- Use with `/analyze-performance` to understand performance metrics
- Use with `/test` to understand testing results and quality metrics

### During Retrospective
- Use with `/understand` to understand complex issues and challenges
- Use with `/debug` to understand root causes of problems
- Use with `/plan` to plan improvement actions

### Post-Retrospective
- Use with `/implement` to implement improvement actions
- Use with `/test` to validate improvement implementations
- Use with `/plan` to plan future improvements and changes

**Remember**: The `/retro` command is your continuous improvement engine. Use it to learn from development experiences, identify improvements, and continuously enhance development processes. The command adapts to your specific needs and provides the level of retrospective detail appropriate for your context.
